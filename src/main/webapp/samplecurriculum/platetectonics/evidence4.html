It's All About Location


Image courtesy of USGS

What is the significance of the location of volcanoes, earthquakes, and mountains? Why do they happen where they do? Most earthquakes and volcanic eruptions do not strike randomly, but occur in specific areas. In addition, mountains are not randomly distributed either. Geological features and events happen at places on the Earth's crust where the plates meet, or plate boundaries.


First, let's take a step back and look at continents. Why are they located where they are? Can you find North America on the map? Where is Africa? South America? Do the continents line up perfectly with the plate boundaries (the thick black lines on the map)?
